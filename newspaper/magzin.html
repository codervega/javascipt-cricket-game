The Indian Economy From 2000-2021

This Section basically tells about The indian Economy from 2000 to 2023
Since gaining independence in 1947, India's economic history can be characterized by distinct phases. The initial decades saw a focus on socialist policies with the public sector playing a dominant role. In 1991, a landmark economic liberalization marked a shift towards market-oriented reforms, encouraging foreign investment and privatization. The Information Technology boom in the late 20th century positioned India as a global player in technology and services. Economic growth surged in the early 2000s, driven by sectors like IT and telecommunications. The mid-2000s witnessed infrastructure development projects and increased globalization. The 2008 global financial crisis had a limited impact due to prudent economic policies. Post-2010, India faced economic challenges, including a slowdown, but reforms such as GST and Make in India were introduced to spur growth. The demonetization move in 2016 aimed at curbing black money. The COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 posed a significant economic challenge, met with relief measures and a focus on self-reliance through the Atmanirbhar Bharat initiative. The economic narrative post-2020 involves recovery, digital transformation, and structural reforms to ensure long-term growth.
